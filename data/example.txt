The astronaut floated silently through the darkness of space.
Stars shimmered around him like ancient, watching eyes.
He thought of Earth — blue, fragile, impossibly far away.
Memories of home flickered: his daughter’s laughter, the smell of rain.
Suddenly, a burst of static pierced his radio — a voice.
“Commander Hayes, do you copy?” it repeated, faint but clear.
He fumbled with the controls, heart pounding.
Somewhere, against the cold silence, someone was reaching back.
